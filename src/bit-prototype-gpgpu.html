### `GPGPU` Developed [`gl-gpgpu`](https://epok.tech/gl-gpgpu){target="_blank"}, a [**General-Processing GPU** or `GPGPU`](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General-purpose_computing_on_graphics_processing_units) tool – useful for computationally intensive tasks like real-time physics and particle systems, of which **Locus** has many. Provides equity of access to **Locus**, with high-performance parallel-computation of complex systems not reliant on the availability of compute-shaders or [`WebGPU`](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/WebGPU_API) – just [`WebGL 1.0`](https://www.khronos.org/webgl/), available on most devices audiences may own personally, not only high-end devices in custom installations. Developed for optimal computation on the `GPU` via [`WebGL 1.0`](https://www.khronos.org/webgl/) and [`GLSL`](https://www.khronos.org/opengl/wiki/Core_Language_(GLSL)), and published [Open](https://www.npmjs.com/package/@epok.tech/gl-gpgpu){target="_blank"}-[Source](https://github.com/keeffEoghan/gl-gpgpu){target="_blank"}.
